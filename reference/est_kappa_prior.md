# Estimate Prior Distribution for Environmental Half-Saturation Constant (kappa)

This function compiles a meta-analysis of published *Vibrio cholerae*
infectious dose (ID50) estimates and fits a lognormal prior distribution
for the environmental dose-response half-saturation constant \\\kappa\\
used in the MOSAIC environmental force-of-infection term: \$\$\lambda_W
\propto W / (\kappa + W)\$\$ where \\W\\ is the environmental bacterial
concentration and \\\kappa\\ is the concentration (CFU) at which the
probability of infection is 50\\

## Usage

``` r
est_kappa_prior(PATHS)
```

## Arguments

- PATHS:

  A list of paths generated by
  [`get_paths()`](https://institutefordiseasemodeling.github.io/MOSAIC-pkg/reference/get_paths.md).
  Must include:

  - **MODEL_INPUT**: directory where parameter CSVs are saved.

  - **DOCS_FIGURES**: directory where the summary figure is saved.

## Value

Invisibly returns a list with elements:

- `data`: the source meta-analysis table.

- `fit`: fitted lognormal parameters (`meanlog`, `sdlog`, `median`,
  `mode`, `ci_lower`, `ci_upper`).

- `param_df`: long-format parameter data frame (as saved to disk).

## Details

The function assembles a literature table of ID50 estimates spanning
direct human-volunteer challenge studies (Hornick 1971, Cash 1974,
Levine 1981/1988, Tacket 1999), QMRA Beta-Poisson fits (Haas, Rose &
Gerba 1999), expert-review summaries (Nelson et al. 2009, Kaper et al.
1995), and values adopted by convention in the cholera modelling
literature (Codeco 2001, Hartley et al. 2006). Each source is weighted
by its relevance to the endemic / buffered exposure context most
relevant to sub-Saharan African transmission settings. A lognormal is
then fit by weighted maximum-likelihood on the log10 scale.

The MOSAIC dose-response follows Codeco (2001) and Hartley et al.
(2006): the infection hazard is a saturating Michaelis-Menten function
of environmental concentration with half-saturation \\\kappa\\.
Historical cholera models have fixed \\\kappa = 10^6\\ cells, a value
traceable to Sack (1971) and Hornick et al. (1971) under unbuffered
water-only exposure in healthy adults. Modern re-analyses of
buffered-exposure volunteer studies (NaHCO3 neutralisation,
representative of typical endemic exposure through food or contaminated
drinking water) consistently support a lower central estimate of \\\sim
10^5\\ CFU, with substantial between-study heterogeneity.

Fitting: each source contributes a point estimate \\\log\_{10}
\kappa_i\\ with weight \\w_i\\ reflecting its evidentiary quality.
Weighted mean and standard deviation on the log10 scale are converted to
lognormal `meanlog` and `sdlog`. The resulting prior is reconciled with
[`fit_lognormal_from_ci()`](https://institutefordiseasemodeling.github.io/MOSAIC-pkg/reference/fit_lognormal_from_ci.md)
so that `meanlog` is consistent with the weighted mode and the 2.5th /
97.5th percentiles match the empirical spread.
